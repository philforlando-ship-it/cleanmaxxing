__Sample Meal Plans & Food Prep__

*Day\-by\-day plans with macros at four calorie tiers, shopping lists, and prep instructions*

__Macros Do Not Change Bodies — Repeated Meals Do__

The failure in nutrition is almost never knowledge\. Most people know what they should eat\. The failure is execution: what to actually buy, how to prep it without turning nutrition into a second job, and what to eat when the plan falls apart\. This document delivers actual day\-by\-day meal plans with macros, per\-tier shopping lists, and prep instructions — not just principles\.

__Choose Your Tier__

1,800 calories: aggressive cut targeting fat loss, six to ten weeks maximum before reassessing\. 2,200 calories: moderate cut or maintenance at lower activity\. 2,600 calories: maintenance at moderate to high activity or lean bulk\. 3,000 calories: lean bulk at moderate to high activity\. All tiers anchor protein at 180g minimum per day\. Remaining calories flex across carbohydrates and fat\.

Snacks \(400 cal | 45P / 20C / 10F\): 1 cup low\-fat cottage cheese, 10 baby carrots, 1 hard boiled egg\. Day totals: 1,800 cal | 170P / 140C / 42F\.

__Day 3__

Breakfast \(420 cal | 40P / 30C / 12F\): 4 whole eggs scrambled with 1/2 cup salsa, 1 slice whole grain toast, black coffee\. Lunch \(440 cal | 42P / 40C / 8F\): 170g rotisserie chicken \(breast, skin removed\), pre\-washed salad bag, 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, 1 tablespoon olive oil and vinegar\. __Batch Cooking Protocol — Under One Hour__

Sunday prep covering five days of the plan above\. Start the rice first \(2 cups dry = approximately 6 cups cooked, covers three days at 1 cup per meal\)\. While rice cooks \(30 min unattended\): season and roast one sheet pan of vegetables at 400°F — broccoli, peppers, zucchini \(25 min, flip once\)\. While both are cooking: cook 1\-1\.2kg of chicken breast in a large pan or in the oven at 400°F until internal temperature reaches 165°F \(25\-30 min\)\. Hard boil 8\-10 eggs simultaneously \(12 min, then ice bath\)\. Final 10 minutes: portion snacks into individual containers — Greek yogurt with measured granola, cottage cheese portions, fruit servings\. Label containers with the day\. Active cooking time under 20 minutes\. Everything stores refrigerated for four to five days\. Freeze the portion for day five or six if needed\.

__Swap Lists by Function__

Lean protein swaps \(per 30g protein serving\): 120g chicken breast, 130g turkey breast, 160g canned tuna \(1 can\), 140g salmon, 150g white fish, 5 large eggs, 1\.5 cups Greek yogurt \(plain\), 3/4 cup cottage cheese, 170g extra\-firm tofu, 1 cup cooked edamame, 1 cup cooked lentils, 1 scoop whey protein\. Carbohydrate swaps \(per 50g carb serving\): 2/3 cup dry oats, 1/3 cup dry rice \(yielding ~1 cup cooked\), 1 medium sweet potato, 1 medium banana, 1 large wrap \(check label — varies significantly\), 2 slices whole grain bread, 2 rice cakes plus fruit\. Wrap vs bread: a standard medium tortilla wrap is 120\-180 calories; two thick slices of bread is 240\-320 calories before any filling\. For daily recurring lunches, wraps or rice\-based bowls are consistently lower calorie without reducing satiety\.

__Restaurant and Travel Fallbacks__

Restaurant default: identify the protein source first, order it grilled or baked, add a vegetable side, skip or minimize bread and chips\. Mexican — grilled chicken or steak bowl over rice, black beans, salsa, skip the sour cream and minimize cheese\. Japanese — sashimi, sushi rolls with protein\-forward fillings, miso soup, edamame\. American — any grilled protein with a vegetable side\. Italian — grilled protein with a salad, or pasta with protein but a half portion of pasta\. Thai — any protein\-forward stir\-fry ordered light on oil\. The rule: the protein source determines the meal quality; everything else is secondary\.

Travel survival kit: whey protein packets \(single\-serve\), beef jerky \(high protein, portable\), individual Greek yogurt cups \(gas stations and convenience stores stock these\), hard boiled eggs \(available at most gas stations\), protein bars with 20g\+ protein and under 300 calories\. These four items cover any situation where food environment is entirely outside your control\.

__Cut Without Looking Depleted — Bulk Without Looking Puffy__

On a cut \(1,800\-2,200 tier\): the depleted look comes from insufficient protein causing muscle loss, aggressive restriction causing chronic fatigue, and very low sodium plus very low carbohydrates producing a flat appearance\. Maintain protein at minimum 180g\. Keep sodium moderate rather than severely restricting — the face retains less water from adequate hydration with moderate sodium than from dehydration\. Time carbohydrates around training to maintain session quality\. Avoid extending the cut below 1,600 calories without medical supervision\. A gaunt face during a cut is a protein and training quality problem, not a fat loss problem\.

On a lean bulk \(2,600\-3,000 tier\): the puffy look comes from excess surplus driving fat gain, particularly visible in the face\. A 150\-300 calorie surplus is sufficient for muscle gain — larger surpluses accelerate fat gain without proportionally accelerating muscle gain\. Monitor the face weekly using the standardized photo protocol in Doc 53\. If facial definition is declining over two to three weeks, reduce the surplus by 200 calories and reassess\.

__A meal plan only works if it is actually executed\. The three\-day plans above are boring by design — repeatable, prep\-friendly, and calibrated to within 50 calories per day\. Pick your tier, follow the shopping list, batch cook once on Sunday, and use the fallback protocols when the plan breaks down\. Adjust the tier based on four\-week weight trends, not daily fluctuations\. Macros are a tool; consistency is the mechanism\.__
